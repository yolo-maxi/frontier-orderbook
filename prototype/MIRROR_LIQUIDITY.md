# Mirror Liquidity (experimental)

Mirror liquidity is a pooled, oracle-free inventory that **mirrors real fills**
on top of the existing two-sided Frontier book. It adds depth a taker can hit
without adding any independent price discovery: it only ever trades at the price
the real book just printed, and only up to the size the real fill produced.

## Why

A resting maker both *discovers price* (commits to a level and bears adverse
selection) and *provides depth*. Mirror liquidity unbundles those: it provides
depth only, riding the real book's price. That makes the book look and fill
deeper without asking mirror LPs to take a view on price. The protocol's stance
is therefore that mirror depth is not entitled to maker economics — see Fees.

## Mechanism

Storage (in `FrontierBookBase`): a single pooled AMM-style position —
`mirrorReserve0`, `mirrorReserve1`, `mirrorTotalShares`, and per-LP
`mirrorShares`.

- **`depositMirror` / `withdrawMirror`** (in `UniformMakerOps`): pro-rata pool
  ops at the pool's current reserve ratio. The first deposit sets the ratio;
  later deposits are clipped to it and oracle-free. Shares are minted/burned
  like LP tokens. No price input is ever taken.
- **Mirror on sweep** (in `UniformFrontierBook.sweepWithLimits`): after the real
  sweep computes `(paid, received)`, the book mirrors that fill against mirror
  inventory:
  - **Up-sweep (ask):** mirror matches the real token0 output 1:1 at the book
    price, capped by `mirrorReserve0`. The taker pays the mirrored token1; the
    pool gains it (net of fee). `received` and `paid` both roughly double.
  - **Down-sweep (bid):** mirror buys token0 (`mirrorReserve0 +=`) and pays
    token1 (`mirrorReserve1 -=`), capped by `mirrorReserve1`.

The mirror is always at the **book price** — `mirrorPaid = realPaid *
mirrorOut / realOut` — so it introduces no new price level and the real maker's
claim is completely unchanged (`claimable` / `bidClaimable` are real-only).

## Fees — mirror pays full, earns no maker discount

`MIRROR_FEE_BPS` (currently 30 bps, a `public constant`) is charged on the
**token1 leg** of every mirror-mirrored fill and routed to the protocol
`feeRecipient` (event `MirrorFee`). It is **never retained by the pool**.

Rationale, and the economic intuition behind the experiment:

- Mirror depth mirrors real price discovery without contributing any, so it pays
  the full rate and is excluded from maker treatment (no maker-fee waiver, no
  rebate eligibility).
- That fee is pure additional protocol revenue generated by volume that would
  not otherwise route through real makers. The protocol can recycle it into
  **lower maker fees / maker rebates**, so honest price-discovering makers
  effectively pay less. Mirror LPs still profit — they earn the book spread on
  the mirrored size — they just do not also capture a fee.
- The taker pays the standard taker fee (`takerFeeBps`) on the **combined**
  real+mirror notional via the normal `_takerTotal` path, so mirror volume is
  taker-taxed at the full rate too; it gets no taker discount for being
  synthetic depth.

Configurability: the rate is a constant for this experiment. If `feeRecipient`
is unset (`address(0)`), the mirror fee is zero and the pool mirrors at pure
book price — fee-less books keep their old behavior and never transfer to
`address(0)`. Productionizing this would promote `MIRROR_FEE_BPS` to an
immutable fee-config field alongside `makerFeeBps` / `takerFeeBps`.

## Documented shortcuts / tradeoffs

These are deliberate simplifications for the experiment:

1. **Budget split is a flat halving.** When mirror inventory is live, the real
   sweep is given `grossBudget / 2` and the mirror takes the rest. This caps
   total spend at the taker's budget in a single pass (no second sweep), but it
   halves the real fill *even when mirror inventory is tiny*, leaving taker
   budget unused in that case. A production version would size the reservation
   to actual mirror inventory (or do a measured two-pass).
2. **Single pooled position, both sides.** Mirror is one global AMM-style pool,
   not per-tick or per-LP ranges. Simple and O(1), but LPs cannot express a
   price view or a range; they take whatever the book prints.
3. **Mirror capped by reserve, no partial-level accounting.** The mirror fills
   `min(reserve, realOut)`; if inventory is short, the mirror contribution is
   simply smaller. No attempt is made to partially mirror a single level with
   level-precise rounding.
4. **Fee always settles in token1.** Both directions carve the mirror fee from
   the token1 (quote) leg for a single, uniform fee path. Economically clean
   for a quote-denominated fee; a base-denominated fee would need a second path.
5. **Gas:** a mirror sweep adds one extra ERC20 transfer (the fee to
   `feeRecipient`) versus a real-only sweep. See `FrontierMirror.t.sol` gas
   probes (`testGasMirrorSweep50Levels` vs `testGasRealOnlySweep50Levels`).

## Tests

`test/FrontierMirror.t.sol` covers: pro-rata deposit/withdraw, ask mirror, bid
mirror, finite-budget split, the no-recipient fee-less path, and gas probes for
real-only vs mirror sweeps in both directions.
